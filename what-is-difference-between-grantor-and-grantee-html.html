Answer

A grantee is a legal entity that has been granted ownership of a piece of real land. The grantee is the person who purchases the grant. The grantor and grantee in a land transaction, for example, are both parties to the deal. The grantor is the owner of the property, and the grantee is the buyer who is getting an equitable interest in the property, rather than a mere legal right in the same.
